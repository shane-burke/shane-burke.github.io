The human cost of organic salmon

Driven by its fishing industry, Alaska has the highest rate of workplace fatalities

By SHANE BURKE

Published June 29, 2021

According to data from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the state with the most workplace fatalities per hundred thousand is Alaska. 7 in 100,000 Alaskans will die at work each year.

This is driven by the state's outsized fishing industry. Fishing is one of the most fatal industries in the United States, according to the same annual census by the Bureau of Labor Statistics. Fishermen spend the winter and spring months on huge ships catching salmon, crab and other seafood. The waters are frigid and turbulent.
